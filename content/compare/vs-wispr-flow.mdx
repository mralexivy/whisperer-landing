Wispr Flow and Whisperer take fundamentally different approaches to dictation. Wispr Flow uses cloud-based AI to rewrite and format your speech. Whisperer processes everything offline on your Mac. Which approach is right for you?

## The Core Difference

**Whisperer:** Your voice is recorded, transcribed by a local Whisper model, and inserted into the active app. Nothing leaves your Mac.

**Wispr Flow:** Your voice is sent to cloud servers for transcription and AI processing. The AI reformats your speech into polished text.

This isn't a minor distinction — it affects privacy, reliability, speed, and cost.

## Where Wispr Flow Wins

### AI Rewriting
Wispr Flow's AI can transform stream-of-consciousness speech into polished, structured text. If you tend to ramble and want clean output, this is genuinely useful.

### Cross-Platform
Wispr Flow supports Mac and Windows. Whisperer is Mac-only.

### Vibe Coding Content
Wispr Flow has positioned itself in the "vibe coding" space with content and features geared toward AI-assisted development workflows.

## The Privacy Question

<Callout type="warning">
When you dictate with Wispr Flow, consider what you're sending to the cloud:
- Source code and technical discussions
- Business emails and confidential messages
- Client names, project details, financial figures
- Passwords or sensitive information mentioned verbally

With Whisperer, none of this leaves your device.
</Callout>

## The Verdict

<Callout type="tip">
**For privacy-conscious users:** Whisperer, without question. 100% offline processing means your data stays yours.

**For developers:** Whisperer's Code Mode and offline processing make it the better choice.

**For budget-conscious users:** Whisperer at $14.99 one-time vs. $120–180/year is dramatically cheaper.

**For users who want AI rewriting:** Wispr Flow's cloud AI can produce more polished output from rough dictation. If this feature is essential and privacy is less of a concern, Wispr Flow may be worth the subscription.
</Callout>
