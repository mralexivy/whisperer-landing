Wispr Flow and Whisperer have opposite architectures. Wispr Flow sends your audio to the cloud for AI processing. Whisperer runs everything [on your Mac](/offline-dictation-mac).

## The Basic Difference

**Whisperer:** Record, transcribe locally, insert text. Nothing leaves your Mac.

**Wispr Flow:** Send audio to servers, get polished text back.

This changes everything: privacy, speed, reliability, cost, whether it works offline.

### Three Engines
[Whisper, NVIDIA, Apple Speech](/features/offline-transcription). Switch without restarting. Wispr Flow has one cloud backend. NVIDIA on Neural Engine is fastest on Apple Silicon.

### Offline AI
[Ten AI modes](/features/ai-writing): rewrite, translate, format, summarize, fix grammar. Not GPT-4 quality, but runs without internet and keeps everything local.

## Where Wispr Flow Wins

### Better AI Rewriting
Cloud AI uses GPT-class models. Better rewrites than on-device models can do. If you dictate messy thoughts and want polished output, Wispr Flow does that better.

### Windows
Mac and Windows. Whisperer is Mac only.

### Marketing
Good "vibe coding" positioning. Developer-focused content.

## The Privacy Question

<Callout type="warning">
With Wispr Flow, your audio goes to servers. Think about what you dictate:
- Source code
- Business emails
- Client names, project details, financials
- Anything sensitive

With Whisperer, none of that leaves your Mac.
</Callout>

## Which One

<Callout type="tip">
**Whisperer if:**
- Privacy matters (offline, nothing leaves your Mac)
- You write code (Code Mode)
- Budget matters ($14.99 once vs $120-180/year)
- You need offline reliability
- You want per-app profiles

**Wispr Flow if:**
- You want GPT-4-class rewriting and are okay with cloud
- You need Windows
- You'll pay $10-15/month for better AI
- You don't need Code Mode
</Callout>
