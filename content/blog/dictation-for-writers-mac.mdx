Professional writers produce 1,000–5,000 words per day. At 50 WPM typing speed, that's 20–100 minutes of pure typing. Speaking at 140 WPM, the same output takes 7–36 minutes. Voice dictation doesn't just save time — it changes how you write.

## Why Writers Should Dictate

### Speed: 3x Faster First Drafts

The numbers are straightforward:

| Output | Typing (50 WPM) | Speaking (140 WPM) | Time Saved |
|--------|-----------------|---------------------|------------|
| Blog post (1,500 words) | 30 min | 11 min | 19 min |
| Newsletter (800 words) | 16 min | 6 min | 10 min |
| Book chapter (3,000 words) | 60 min | 21 min | 39 min |
| Daily journal (500 words) | 10 min | 4 min | 6 min |

<Callout type="tip">
These are **first draft** times. Most writers edit extensively after drafting. Voice dictation front-loads the creative output, so you spend more time refining and less time producing raw text.
</Callout>

### Flow State: Speaking Is More Natural

Writing by typing engages your fingers and eyes simultaneously, creating a feedback loop that encourages self-editing as you write. This slows the creative process.

Speaking bypasses this loop. You close your eyes (or look away from the screen), speak your thoughts, and let the words flow. Many writers report that dictated first drafts are more natural and conversational — closer to their authentic voice.

### Reduced Physical Strain

Writers are at high risk for RSI, carpal tunnel, and back problems from extended typing sessions. Voice dictation eliminates the physical strain of typing while maintaining — or increasing — output. See our [RSI prevention guide](/blog/rsi-prevention-voice-coding/) for more.

## Writing Workflows with Whisperer

### The Brain Dump Method

1. Hold Fn and speak everything on your mind about the topic
2. Don't worry about structure, grammar, or coherence
3. Release — Whisperer transcribes your stream of consciousness
4. Use AI Rewrite mode on the result to clean it up
5. Edit and structure the polished text

This method is powerful because it separates creation from editing. You produce raw material first, then refine it.

### The Section-by-Section Method

1. Outline your article with section headers
2. For each section, hold Fn and dictate that section's content
3. Move to the next section and repeat
4. Review and edit the complete draft

This method works well for structured content like blog posts, articles, and documentation.

### The Walking Draft

1. Put on headphones (for microphone access)
2. Open Whisperer in toggle mode (press to start, press to stop)
3. Walk and dictate your draft
4. Return to your desk with a complete first draft

Walking boosts creative thinking by 60% according to Stanford research. Combined with voice dictation, it's the ultimate writing productivity hack.

## Tips for Better Writing Dictation

1. **Speak in complete thoughts** — pause between sentences, not mid-sentence
2. **Use the Rewrite shortcut** (Option+Shift+Tab) to clean up any selected text
3. **Dictate section by section** rather than the entire piece at once
4. **Add punctuation naturally** — say "period," "comma," "new paragraph" as you go
5. **Build your dictionary** — add character names, location names, and domain-specific terms
6. **Use filler word removal** — enable in Settings to strip "um," "uh," "so" automatically
