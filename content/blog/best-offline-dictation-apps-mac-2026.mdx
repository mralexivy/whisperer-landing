## The Contenders

### 1. Whisperer

**Price:** Free (Pro Pack: $14.99 one-time) | **Engine:** whisper.cpp (on-device)

Whisperer is a menu-bar app that inserts text into any focused field. Hold Fn, speak, release — text appears where your cursor is. The Pro Pack adds Code Mode (camelCase, snake_case, symbols by voice), per-app profiles, and a personal dictionary.

<Callout type="tip">
Whisperer's Code Mode is unique in the market — no other Mac dictation app can handle camelCase, snake_case, and programming symbols by voice.
</Callout>

### 2. Superwhisper

**Price:** $249 lifetime (or $8/mo) | **Engine:** Whisper (local + cloud options)

Superwhisper offers various transcription modes with optional AI processing. Well-designed with strong brand recognition, but expensive and some modes use cloud processing.

### 3. MacWhisper

**Price:** ~$64 one-time | **Engine:** whisper.cpp

MacWhisper is primarily a **transcription** tool — designed for transcribing audio files and recordings, not for live dictation into other apps.

### 4. VoiceInk

**Price:** One-time purchase | **Engine:** Whisper (on-device)

VoiceInk is a straightforward Mac dictation app with a focus on simplicity. Clean design but limited advanced features.

### 5. Apple Dictation (Built-in)

**Price:** Free | **Engine:** Apple Neural Engine

Apple's built-in Dictation has improved significantly and now runs on-device. But it still has aggressive autocorrection, no developer features, and no per-app control.
