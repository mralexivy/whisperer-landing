Email is mostly typing the same kinds of sentences over and over. "Thanks for sending this." "Let me know if you have questions." "Can we schedule a call?" Speaking these feels faster than typing them, and it is.

## Why This Works

Typing: 40-60 words per minute. Speaking: 130-150. Email is natural language, not code. No symbols. Just talk.

<Callout type="info">
A 100-word email takes:
- **Typing:** 1.5-2.5 minutes
- **Dictation:** 40-50 seconds

If you send 40 emails a day, that adds up to **40-80 minutes**.
</Callout>

## Works Anywhere

Whisperer inserts text wherever your cursor is. Any email client works:

- **Gmail** (Chrome, Safari, Arc)
- **Apple Mail**
- **Outlook**
- **Superhuman**
- **Hey**

### Set Up a Profile for Email

Create a per-app profile with natural language mode (not Code Mode). Whisperer switches automatically when your email app is in front.

You get proper sentences with punctuation for email, while Code Mode stays active for your IDE.

## What Works Best

<StepList>
<Step num="1" title="Speak naturally">
Whisperer picks up punctuation from your pauses and tone
</Step>

<Step num="2" title="Dictate the whole thing at once">
Sentence by sentence is slower than just saying the whole email
</Step>

<Step num="3" title="Scan before sending">
Takes two seconds. Catches the occasional transcription error.
</Step>

<Step num="4" title="Add names to your dictionary">
Colleague names, project names, company jargon. Saves correction time.
</Step>
</StepList>

## Automatic Mode Switching

<Callout type="tip">
Per-app profiles mean you don't think about modes:

| App | Mode | Result |
|-----|------|--------|
| Gmail | Natural language | "Hi Sarah, can we reschedule our 3pm?" |
| VS Code | Code Mode | `const fetchUserData = async () => {` |
| Slack | Chat style | "sounds good, I'll take a look" |
| Terminal | Code Mode | `git push origin main` |

Whisperer sees which app is active and adjusts.
</Callout>

## Mobile Drafts

Whisperer is Mac-only, but most email clients sync drafts. Start an email by voice on your Mac, finish on your phone. Or the other way around.
